Before the Horn Blows

The Blowing of the Horn

“And the Trumpet will be blown, and all who are in the heavens and all who are on the earth will swoon away, except him whom Allah wills” (Qur'an 39:68)
"It is He who created the heavens and the earth in true (proportions): the day He saith, "Be," behold! it is. His word is the truth. His will be the dominion the day the trumpet will be blown. He knoweth the unseen as well as that which is open. For He is the Wise, well acquainted (with all things)." (Qur'an 6:73)
"And when the trumpet is blown with a single blast, And the earth and the mountains are borne away and dissolved like powder.... On that day you shall be exposed to view - no secret of yours shall remain hidden." (Qur'an 69:13-17)

The first blowing of the trumpet then transforms everything. The “veil” of the physical world is removed with the first blowing.

The second blowing is the resurrection. The verse says, "....and lord, they are standing and are looking on! And the earth shines forth with the Light of its Lord." (39:68-69)

The Messenger (PBUH) has told us that the one who will blow the Trumpet has been ready to do so since Allah created him. The question is are YOU ready for the day of judgement?

The following are some of these signs:

  • Death of the scholars

  • Widespread murder

  • Prevalence of ignorance

  • Trials everywhere

  • Frequent earthquakes

  • Following the ways of the disbelievers

  • Abundance of money

  • The spread of sins, usury and adultery

  • The Bedouins and the erstwhile poor nations will be so rich to the extent that they will become boastful

What to do?

A good Muslim has a strong solid faith, which gives the person and the people around him inner and outer strength.

  1. Believe that Allah the almighty exists. Also accept that Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) is the last of a long line of Prophets. Accept that the Holy Quran is the most recent and true word of Allah. Never believe what the media portrays about Muslims around the world, individuals or groups in general.

  2. Pray! Always pray on time, never delay prayer time not even more MIST competitions. Never do things that will prevent you from doing tasks that are of much greater importance. Nothing can be more important than obeying that which Allah asks of you. Even while working or studying, halt everything you are doing and go to the nearest mosque to pray. The key to success starts from praying 5 times a day.

  3. Pray in the middle of the night. Pray when most people around you are sleeping soundly. The name of the prayer is Tahajjud. The night is the most efficacious time to pray.

  4. Recite his name every time. Also known as Zikr, reciting Allah's name will make you a strong and better Muslim because you are constantly reminded of what good he has done for you and all of humanity.

  5. Be grateful for everything you have, be it spiritual, mental, or physical. The more gracious you are, the more you will realize how much you have been blessed.

  6. Protect your chastity. You must know that adultery is a sinful act, and thus must always avoid it. This applies to both men and women. Men and Women should not wear tight or revealing clothing, and both should always lower their gaze.

  7. Be the best to your family, The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) said, "The best of you is he who is the best to his family."

  8. Always think right and Halal. Never let evil ideas come into your mind.

  9. Ask Allah for forgiveness if you have committed a sin. Allah shall forgive you, In Sha Allah. Don’t repeat the same sin again. The acceptance to paradise is worth more than anything.
