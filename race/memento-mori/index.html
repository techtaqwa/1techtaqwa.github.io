Memento Mori

Life. Death. Time.

This too shall pass

It is said an Eastern monarch once charged his wise men to invent him a sentence, to be ever in view, and which should be true and appropriate in all times and situations. They presented him the words: "And this, too, shall pass away." How much it expresses! How chastening in the hour of pride! How consoling in the depths of affliction!

Ozymandias

Percy B. Shelly

I met a traveller from an antique land,

Who said—“Two vast and trunkless legs of stone

Stand in the desert. . . . Near them, on the sand,

Half sunk a shattered visage lies, whose frown,

And wrinkled lip, and sneer of cold command,

And wrinkled lip, and sneer of cold command,

Which yet survive, stamped on these lifeless things,

The hand that mocked them, and the heart that fed;

And on the pedestal, these words appear:

My name is Ozymandias, King of Kings;

Look on my Works, ye Mighty, and despair!

Nothing beside remains. Round the decay

Of that colossal Wreck, boundless and bare

The lone and level sands stretch far away.”

Remeber your mortality

In both of the pieces above, the moral of the story revolved around the passing of time. This too shall pass was a phrase which reminded that particular monarch of his mortality and how finite his life and power was. In Ozymandias, the traveler came across a ruin which was supposedly built on the orders of an ancient King, who thought himself to be the King of Kings and all powerful. But his time has long since passed and he is now forgotten, leaving the crumbling ruins as the only evidence of his power.

Time will keep passing no matter who you are. All your possessions, wealth, and power are temporary and will one day pass away. With all these worldly possessions, it's easy to forget time and your mortality. But death is something that shouldn't be forgotten. Falling for worldly desires is a dangerous thing, when we all could be using that time to stack up good deeds and gain reward in the hereafter. In this life there is only one truth, and that truth is the truth of death.

Memento mori, remember your mortality.
