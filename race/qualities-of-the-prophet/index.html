Qualities of the Prophet

In 570 AD, a boy named Muhammad was born to the Banu Hashim tribe in Mecca. He was Muhammad, son of Abdullah and Ameena. He was chosen by Allah, to be the last of messengers to guide mankind from turning away from Allah.

As a young child, he had good manners and spoke clearly and politely. And as he grew up and became a young man, he displayed the result of his good upbringing. He spent his time working for his uncle in the pastures outside of Mecca. He was illiterate and had no education or schooling, but he would try to learn as much as he could.

The Prophet Muhammad was the best of all mankind. He displayed all the characteristics that all people-not just Muslims should embody. To live a full life of righteousness and sincerity, we must follow the example of Muhammad pbuh. Who else would be a greater role model? By learning about our beloved Prophet and following his Sunnah, we can fulfill the time we spend wisely in this world.

He was generous, trustworthy, compassionate, patient, honest, humble, and merciful.
