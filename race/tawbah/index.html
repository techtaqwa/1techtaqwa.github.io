Time for Tawbah

The meaning of tawbah is to return and to return from the far side to the near side. It has a beginning and an end. The beginning (Ibtidaa) of tawbah is the spreading of the rays of Noor-e-Ma'rifat (the Light of Divine Recognition) of the heart which thus realizes that the sin committed is a fatal poison which wroughts great spiritual disaster. This realization induces regret and fear which result in a true and sincere yearning to compensate for the sin. This yearning is to such a degree that shunning of the sin is immediate. Further, a firm resolve and intention are made to totally abstain from the sin in future. Along with this intention a full effort is made to compensate for the past shortcoming. When the result is this fruit of taubah pertaining to the past, present and future, then perfection of tawbah has been acquired. This then is the end (Intihaa) of taubah.

We have all sinned and we will continue to sin, but the best of sinners are those who repent. Keep this question in mind, how can I stand in front of Allah with an account of everything I’ve done?

“With every difficulty comes ease” (94:5-6)
“Every son of Adam is a sinner.”- Mohammad (PBUH)
“Believers, respond to God and His Messenger when he calls you to that which gives you life...” (8:24)

The first thing that the Quran emphasizes is the relationship between piety (being God conscious) and forgiveness. For example in (3:134) it describes a true believer “Those who spend (freely), whether in prosperity, or in adversity; who restrain anger, and pardon (all) men;- for Allah loves those who do good. The Quran makes a connection between forgiveness (on our part) and our need for God’s forgiveness. For example in (24:22) “Let them forgive and overlook, do you not wish that Allah should forgive you?” In other words if one wants God to forgive their sins then they should be understanding to other people’s difficulties and forgive them. The Quran also ties between forgiveness and perseverance. For example we read in the Quran (7:199) “Hold to forgiveness; command what is right; But turn away from the ignorant.”

The strongest weapon that every Muslim has against adversity is Dua (supplication). When you feel hopeless and feel that the world has shut all doors of happiness for you, you know that there is always a source awaiting your call and willing to help you. Currently, most of the Muslim countries are under attack, however, the only thing that keeps them going is the firm faith in Allah and the belief that their Duas will be accepted one day.

We can not just repent when something bad happens in our life. Repent for everything and anything. During this past Ramadan it was our job and duty to ask Allah to forgive all of our sins, all of our wrong doings, accept all of our good deed, ask that whatever you do in the future are only deeds that leads to Jannah.
